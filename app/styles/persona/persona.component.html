Design Persona

Definition

What is a Design Persona?

“Following a structure similar to a user persona, you can flesh out your design’s personality by creating a design persona. Personality can manifest itself in an interface through visual design, copy, and interactions. A design persona describes how to channel personality in each of these areas and helps the web team to construct a unified and consistent result.”
-Aarron Walter in “Designing for Emotion”

The Design Persona is used to create and design a product that engages users emotionally in order to create a positive and memorable experience. This document serves as a framework for making product and design decisions, ensuring that every decision made creates a unified and consistent experience in NaviPlan.

Overview

Who is NaviPlan?

NaviPlan is the wise and trusted mentor of financial planning, a source of powerful information, a comprehensive long-term planner, and a familiar face in a crowd of newcomers.

NaviPlan is an expert full of immense knowledge and power, tasked to better understand the field and bring that value to users. It helps users understand and impact their financial future by providing pragmatic information and analysis of their situation. Ultimately, users are empowered by NaviPlan, an experienced and established tool that speaks with clarity and understanding.

Voice

How does NaviPlan sound?

Voice Overview

NaviPlan is incredibly knowledgeable and confident, yet it does not feel the need to prove this. It does not rely on verbose formality to convey its knowledge, rather, efficiency and clarity are valued over excessively academic vocabulary. NaviPlan understands different users are at different levels of understanding and will tailor its language accordingly to fulfill the ultimate goal of helping people better understand the world. NaviPlan uses a scientific and precise feel, manifesting from the navigational theme found in imagery, differentiating itself from competitors. It never veers into an overly clever territory, instead, NaviPlan is focused on engaging users in conversation and resolving problems to promote an emotional connection.

Copy Examples

When guiding newer or less knowledgeable

NaviPlan uses clear, plain-English explanations and instructions.

"More information is needed before a report can be generated. Fill out your client's information and try again."

"You need to enter an income before continuing."

"The description field contains invalid characters. Only use alphanumeric (A-Z, a-z, 1-9) characters in the description field."
When working with more experienced users or complex topics

NaviPlan is efficient but rigorously precise with explanations and wording.

"Holdings are investment products, such as stocks, bonds, or mutual funds. These can be used to fund a client's goals."
When things are going well

NaviPlan is not afraid to let you know and congratulate you, but it will not dwell on it.

"Your report has been successfully generated."

"Client's information has been updated. Continue?"
When something goes wrong

NaviPlan immediately lets you know that there is a problem, where it is, and how to fix it.

"The error in this field must be fixed before proceeding."

"We need more information about your client before we can generate insights. Try adding your clients’ incomes."

"Continue and remove transfer records? This account is associated with one or more transfer records. Changing either the Account Type or Owner will delete these associated items from your plan. Where applicable, proceeds and distributions will be directed into Cash Flow."

Visual Lexicon

What does NaviPlan look like?

Colors

NaviPlan’s color palette uses more neutral colors with instances of bright colors to convey a sense of professionalism, yet remain inviting and fun for the user. In NaviPlan, there is consistent use of gold and purple colors to emphasize structure. The purpose of these colors is to enable a smooth, intuitive flow through the application.

Typography

NaviPlan is powerful, clear, and efficient, and its typography reflects it. Simple, sans-serif headings and a body copy appropriately varied in scale and weight communicate information hierarchy. The dark grey color and clean lines of Nunito Sans make NaviPlan feel like a trusted analytical tool that is both functional and easy to use.

Illustration

NaviPlan uses elements that are flat and simple, keeping the tool easy to understand, clean, and not intimidating. Illustrations use lines and light color fills to complete a space without making it feel busy and unclear. Purple should be the main color used in the lines and fills of illustrations, gold can be brought in when elements are large enough or in an area that calls for more detail. NaviPlan uses illustrations sparingly and only in areas that can afford an added element, such as empty states or modal selections.

Shadows/Elevation

NaviPlan uses shadows and elevation in elements that overlap other elements on a page when opened, expanded, hovered, etc. Shadows are light and have a high blur, this allows the shadow to elevate the element without making it too distinguished or distracting to the user. This shadow guideline should be followed: -2px y-axis, 0px x-axis, 10px blur, 2px spread, and (0,0,0,6) color. NaviPlan uses shadows and elevation sparingly and only in areas such as dropdown, tooltips, side panels, list buttons, datepickers, and snackbars.

Engagement Methods

How does NaviPlan interact with users?

Surprise & Delight

NaviPlan changes the aerial photography images on login screens each release for both the NaviPlan financial planning tool and the NaviPlan Administration tool.

NaviPlan uses micro-interactions on elements to entice the user to continue working through the tool. These micro-interactions are slight but enough to be noticeable, interesting, and unexpected to the user.

Anticipation

NaviPlan uses Pendo to add Coming Soon and What’s New popups into the tool, allowing the user to anticipate and understand new features, calculations, or design elements.

Velvet Rope & Status

NaviPlan uses Beta or User Testing on chosen user groups for new features, calculations, or design elements. This limited access to new product elements helps elicit a feeling of exclusivity, allowing the user to anticipate and feel an elevated perceived status.
